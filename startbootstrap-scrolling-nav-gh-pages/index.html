Gun Violence In United States

Gun-related violence is violence committed with the use of a gun. Gun-related violence may or may not be considered criminal. According to various respected online sources, Gun-based "Criminal Violence" includes homicide, assault with a deadly weapon, and suicide, or attempted suicide. Thus,the prior all depends on the jurisdiction. On the other hand, the "Non-Criminal Violence" includes accidental or unintentional injury and death! (Except perhaps in cases of criminal negligence). From a different note, gun violence statistics are military or para-military activities.

  • According to, Kaggle and Wikipedia, The United States of America has the 11th highest rate of gun violence in the world. Moreover, USA is the largest of any large or highly developed nation to have the following statistics: "Having a gun homicide rate which is 25 times higher than any other developed nation; furthermore, an unintentional gun death rate which is 6 times higher than any other developed nation; in addition to, a firearm suicide rate which is 8 times higher than any other developed nation. Lastly, an overall firearm death rate which is 10 times higher than the average respective rates of other high income nations.
